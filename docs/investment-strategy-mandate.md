# Investment Strategy Mandate

## 1. Purpose

The AI Portfolio Manager is a future-oriented, high-upside, research-driven, paper-first AI Investment Committee. It is designed to study long-duration technology, infrastructure, and strategic-industrial themes with a professional research workflow and a permanent audit trail.

This mandate defines the intended portfolio identity and guardrails. It does not authorize live trading, brokerage execution, margin, options, crypto/token trading, or any direct automated execution path.

## 2. Portfolio Identity

The system should behave like a supervised investment committee, not a one-shot trading bot. Its default posture is to identify important themes early, turn them into research tasks, challenge the evidence, and only then consider paper-portfolio actions.

The portfolio identity is:

- Future-oriented: focused on multi-year structural shifts.
- High-upside: willing to research aggressive themes and emerging winners.
- Research-driven: every idea needs a source trail, thesis, risks, and review trigger.
- Paper-first: paper portfolio validation comes before any real-money consideration.
- Audit-focused: decisions, objections, risk checks, and post-mortems should be reviewable.

## 3. Core Themes

Primary research themes:

- AI infrastructure
- Hyperscalers and platform companies
- Semiconductors and compute supply chains
- Data centers, networking, cooling, memory, and power infrastructure
- Nuclear energy, uranium, small modular reactors, electrification, and grid infrastructure
- Robotics and automation
- Defense technology, autonomy, drones, space, cybersecurity, and electronic warfare
- Quantum and frontier compute
- Cybersecurity
- Picks-and-shovels suppliers to the above themes

These themes define research direction. They do not override risk limits, evidence standards, or human review.

## 4. Crypto-Adjacent Infrastructure Clarification

The mandate prohibits crypto trading and direct crypto/token exposure.

The system should not ignore public equities that are crypto-adjacent when the research thesis is really about AI compute, high-performance computing, data centers, power capacity, grid interconnects, cooling, or strategic infrastructure.

Bitcoin miners, former crypto miners, and crypto-infrastructure companies may be researched when the thesis is tied to AI infrastructure, HPC infrastructure, data-center conversion, power assets, cooling, or strategic infrastructure. These names must be treated as high-risk AI infrastructure or power infrastructure research candidates, not as crypto trades.

Researching a crypto-adjacent public equity does not authorize token trading, crypto exposure, or direct crypto strategy execution.

## 5. What High Risk / High Reward Means

High risk / high reward means the research agenda can include emerging, volatile, and controversial themes where the upside could be large but the evidence must be challenged hard.

It means:

- More skepticism, not less.
- Explicit downside cases.
- Clear position-size limits in the paper portfolio.
- Strong source-quality checks.
- Required review triggers.
- Ongoing post-mortems against benchmarks and original assumptions.

High risk / high reward is a research mandate. It is not permission for uncontrolled trading.

## 6. What It Does Not Mean

This mandate does not permit:

- Live trading.
- Real-money brokerage execution.
- Margin.
- Options.
- Direct crypto/token trading.
- Automatic execution from agents.
- Direct BUY or SELL recommendations from X/social data.
- Direct BUY or SELL recommendations from document signals.
- Bypassing deterministic risk checks.
- Bypassing human review.
- Using paid data dependencies unless explicitly approved later.

Aggressive themes still need disciplined process.

## 7. Research Workflow

Every investment idea should flow through:

Research -> Skeptic/Challenger -> Portfolio Manager -> deterministic Risk Engine -> Human Review

Expected workflow:

1. Source intake creates research tasks, watchlist items, or report notes.
2. Equity research builds a concise thesis with source trail, catalyst, risks, time horizon, and required verification.
3. Skeptic/Challenger review looks for stale data, weak sourcing, crowded trades, valuation problems, contradictory evidence, liquidity issues, and narrative overreach.
4. Portfolio Manager review may propose paper BUY, SELL, HOLD, or WATCH actions only after objections are recorded.
5. The deterministic Risk Engine enforces hard limits and may reject or resize recommendations.
6. Human review remains the final gate.
7. Post-mortem review tracks whether the idea worked and whether the process was sound.

## 8. Risk and Execution Guardrails

Permanent guardrails:

- Paper portfolio comes first.
- Agents may propose ideas, but deterministic rules and human review control execution.
- No live trading yet.
- No real-money brokerage execution yet.
- No margin.
- No options.
- No direct crypto/token trading.
- No direct trade from X/social data.
- X/social signals may create research tasks, watchlist items, and report notes only.
- Document signals are research-only and cannot create direct BUY or SELL recommendations.
- Deterministic Risk Engine remains the hard risk gate.
- Human review remains permanent.

## 9. Future Real-Money Sandbox Requirements

Any future small real-money sandbox must wait until the paper system proves that it can operate with discipline.

Minimum requirements before even considering a real-money sandbox:

- Stable paper portfolio workflow.
- Reliable audit trail for every recommendation.
- Clear source trail and evidence snippets.
- Deterministic risk gates that reject or resize unsafe actions.
- Performance tracking against relevant benchmarks.
- Post-mortems that identify mistakes, overconfidence, and unsupported claims.
- Demonstrated handling of stale, contradictory, or low-quality data.
- Clear evidence that recommendations are not driven directly by X/social hype or unreviewed document signals.
- Explicit human approval for scope, size, asset types, and brokerage setup.

Until those requirements are met, the system remains paper-only.

## 10. Source Intake Philosophy

Source intake should be broad enough to discover interesting ideas and disciplined enough to avoid turning noise into trades.

Accepted source-intake roles:

- Insider, fundamental, document, and social/bookmark data can create research leads.
- X/social signals can flag repeated mentions, themes, catalysts, watchlist items, or report notes.
- Document signals can identify guidance tone, demand commentary, margin pressure, capital allocation, risk factors, or management uncertainty.
- Quant and factor ideas can create hypotheses for validation.

Limits:

- X/social signals cannot directly create BUY or SELL recommendations.
- Document signals cannot directly create BUY or SELL recommendations.
- Quant factors cannot directly create trades.
- Curated source intake should be treated as evidence to verify, not a decision engine.
- Deterministic parsing and metadata should come before expensive model calls.
- LLM calls should be used only where they add useful classification, summarization, or critique.
